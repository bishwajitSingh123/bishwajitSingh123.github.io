About Me

Engineering Philosophy

I believe in building AI systems that work in the real world, not just on clean benchmarks. This means designing models that survive noise, handling edge cases that actually occur in production, and creating reproducible pipelines that other engineers can trust and extend.

My approach centers on three principles: discipline over hype (systematic experimentation beats chasing trends), reproducibility over shortcuts (if it can't be reproduced, it can't be deployed), and clinical realism over benchmark scores (medical AI must work with real patient data, not idealized datasets).

Strong models are built with discipline, not noise. This means rigorous validation, ablation studies to understand what actually matters, and deployment readiness from day one‚Äînot as an afterthought.
